OrganiQ

Our services would be going door to door of customers houses and mass dumping buildings such as colleges, hotels etc. and collecting their food wastages. We will then collect the food waste and process it in our location so that we can convert it into Organic fertilizers. The customers will be registered using an app or a website and their total amount of waste will be kept track of. Based on the amount of waste that they give and the repeating use of our service, they will be given points which they can use to redeem various necessities such as phone recharge, net packs etc. After the conversion, we will also be selling that manure to the registered customers for comparatively lower prices.


Predicting and Recommending Business Potential: Harnessing Artificial Intelligence and Business Intelligence Techniques for Regional Market Analysis

We are exploring the potential of using a combination of artificial intelligence and business intelligence techniques for regional market analysis to predict business potential. The study examines how these technologies can be leveraged to analyze and interpret data from various sources, including demographics, economic indicators, consumer behaviour, and social media, to generate insights and forecasts to aid business decision-making. The paper presents a case study of a regional market analysis conducted using machine learning algorithms and data mining techniques, i.e., OSEMN [Obtain, Scrub, Explore, Manipulate, Interrupt] framework, which yielded valuable information on market trends, customer preferences, and potential areas for investment. The results demonstrate the potential of AI and BI for enhancing Business intelligence and decision-making processes, particularly in the context of regional market analysis.


Quality Score

Defects: This metric measures the number of defects or errors found in a particular project or process. Tracking the number of defects can help identify areas for improvement and allow you to take corrective actions to reduce the number of errors. Error Rate: This metric measures the frequency of errors that occur within a given time period. It can help you identify patterns and trends in error occurrence and take corrective actions to reduce the overall error rate. Sample Rate: This metric measures the percentage of samples tested in a particular process or project. It can help ensure that you are testing enough samples to get an accurate representation of the overall quality of the project or process. Place: This metric refers to the location where the work is being done, which can be useful for identifying patterns or trends in quality issues based on the location where the work is being performed.
